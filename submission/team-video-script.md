# Team video script — 55 seconds, founder on camera, full frame

The organizers asked for four things, in this order of emphasis: who you are, where
you are joining from, what you are studying, what you want to build beyond the
hackathon. Lead with the operations background — it is why you are credible on this
problem. Claim nothing you cannot personally defend.

> Personal facts are placeholders — speak your own, exactly as true.

| t | on camera | content |
|---|---|---|
| 0–8 | Face, natural light, plain background | "I'm [NAME], joining from [CITY]. Before anything academic: I spent [X years] in [operations / manufacturing / site work — the real one], where renting equipment meant calling around and hoping you asked every fee question." |
| 8–18 | — | "I'm currently studying [PROGRAM] at [INSTITUTION]. ProcureCall exists because I've been on the buying end of phone-priced markets: the price you get depends on stamina, not on the market." |
| 18–38 | — | "For the hackathon I built the piece I always wanted: an agent that takes one brief, calls every supplier, and can only cite numbers a server verified. Watching a dispatcher drop ninety euros because the agent had a verified competing quote — that's the product in one moment." |
| 38–55 | — | "Beyond the hackathon I want to take this to real rental desks in [REGION]: real phone calls, a growing graph of verified quotes, and a buyer who never overpays for lack of time. If that future matters to you, the demo is live — procurecall.vercel.app." |

Delivery notes: sentence case energy, no hype words, one take if possible; keep
under 60 s hard.
